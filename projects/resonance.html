Resonance

A climbable, large-scale bamboo queen's conch shell that transforms sound, light, and movement into a shared ritual journey.

Overview

Resonance invites people into deeper awareness through immersive architecture, sound, light, and collective movement.

Rooted in the geometry of the queen's conch shell and built entirely from bamboo, Resonance is designed to be entered, climbed, listened to, and shared. The experience is created collectively rather than observed from a distance — participants spiral through stairs, nets, ladders, and platforms, emerging at the top with a shared sense of place and presence.

The Experience

A place to inhabit, not observe.

Participant Journey

Participants enter beneath an undulating bamboo roof filled with wind chimes, warm light, and reflective shell fragments. Moving inward and upward, they spiral through stairs, nets, ladders, platforms, and crevices — each level opening onto new views, sounds, and sensations. The structure rewards curiosity and physical engagement.

Sound and Atmosphere

The shell form naturally amplifies and transforms ambient sound. Wind, voices, and the resonance of bamboo create a constantly shifting acoustic environment. At key moments — sunrise and sunset — participants are invited to blow conch shells together in a brief coordinated call, creating a shared act of listening that honors natural cycles and collective presence.

Material and Construction

Built entirely from bamboo — one of the most rapidly renewable, high-strength, and carbon-sequestering materials available — Resonance foregrounds a regenerative relationship between architectural ambition and material responsibility. The shell geometry was developed through parametric modeling in Grasshopper and refined in Rhino with engineering and fabrication partners at Bamboo Pure, Bali.

Ritual and Gathering

Resonance is designed for gathering. The interior creates natural spaces for rest, conversation, and ceremony. Its scale and presence invite communities to claim it as their own — a landmark, a meeting place, a shared environment that belongs to everyone who enters it.

Project Story

Origin and Vision

The project grew from Elliot Fabri's vision of the queen's conch shell as a natural architectural form — and from a desire to explore how sound, vibration, geometry, and human experience might converge in a single public environment. The conch shell has carried deep cultural meaning across the Pacific, Caribbean, and beyond: as a call to gather, a symbol of the ocean, a vessel for sound. Resonance takes that meaning and builds it at human scale.

Resonance demonstrates how immersive public art can generate awe, foster connection, and model ecological responsibility. It is designed to travel — adaptable to public art programs, cultural festivals, museum contexts, and immersive architecture commissions — while maintaining the core experience of spatial intimacy and collective presence that defines it.

The project is currently in active fundraising. The team has completed parametric design and structural development and is seeking site partners, funding relationships, and production collaborators to bring the first full-scale installation to life.
