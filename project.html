Resonance

A climbable, large-scale bamboo queen's conch shell that transforms sound, light, and movement into a shared ritual journey.

Overview

A public installation shaped as a living spatial environment.

Resonance invites people into deeper awareness through immersive architecture, sound, light, and movement. Rooted in natural form and participatory design, the structure is meant to be entered, climbed, listened to, and shared. The experience is created collectively rather than observed from a distance.

Project story

Origin

The project grew from Elliot Fabri's vision of the queen's conch shell as a natural architectural form and from a desire to explore how sound, vibration, geometry, and human experience might converge in a single public environment.

Why it matters

Resonance demonstrates how immersive public art can generate awe, foster connection, and model ecological responsibility through regenerative materials, inclusive collaboration, and a participatory experience of space.

Experience

Participant experience

Participants enter beneath an undulating bamboo roof filled with wind chimes, warm light, and reflective shell fragments. Moving inward and upward, they spiral through stairs, nets, ladders, platforms, and crevices.

Ritual experience

At sunrise and sunset, conch shells are blown together in a brief coordinated call, creating a shared act of listening that honors natural cycles, ancestry, and collective presence.

Design and material systems

Structure

The shell geometry was developed through parametric modeling in Grasshopper and refined in Rhino. Structural logic, arch curvature, and fabrication planning were developed in collaboration with engineering and bamboo fabrication partners.

Sustainability

Built in bamboo, Resonance foregrounds a rapidly renewable, strong, and carbon-sequestering material while demonstrating a more regenerative relationship between architectural ambition and material practice.
